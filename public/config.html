What happens?

      
Secure configuration turns risky defaults into explicit controls

      

        

          
Risky defaultSafer controlEffect

          
            
Default/root userDedicated app userLimits blast radius.

            
Broad privilegesLeast privilegeReduces abuse if credentials leak.

            
No TLSTLSProtects traffic in transit.

            
No backupsTested backupsSupports recovery.

            
No monitoringAudit logs and metricsMakes incidents visible.

            
Plain secretsSecret managementSupports rotation and reduces leakage.

          
        

      

      

        The score starts at 100 and subtracts illustrative control weights. Missing critical controls impose a minimum
        risk floor, so hardcoded secrets, root users, public ports, or broad privileges cannot be hidden by unrelated
        good settings.
